Furnace supports a vast collection of sound chips from vintage gaming consoles, home computers, arcade machines, and modern fantasy chips. Each chip has unique characteristics and capabilities that define the sound of different gaming eras.

## What are Sound Chips?

Sound chips (also called audio processors or PSGs - Programmable Sound Generators) are dedicated hardware components that generate audio in retro computers and gaming consoles. Unlike modern systems that use general-purpose audio, these chips have specific architectures that define their unique sound characteristics.

## Complete Chip List

Below is the full list of chips that Furnace supports. Click any chip name to view detailed documentation, effects list, and configuration options.

### FM Synthesis Chips

Yamaha's FM synthesis chips were widely used in arcade machines, home computers, and consoles. They use frequency modulation to create complex, metallic sounds.

- [YM2151 (OPM)](/chips/fm/ym2151-opm) - 8-channel FM chip used in arcade boards and X68000
- [YM2203 (OPN)](/chips/fm/ym2203-opn) - 3 FM channels + 3 SSG channels
- [YM2608 (OPNA)](/chips/fm/ym2608-opna) - Enhanced OPN with ADPCM
- [YM2610 (OPNB)](/chips/fm/ym2610-opnb) - Neo Geo's sound chip
- [YM2612/YM3438 (OPN2)](/chips/fm/ym2612-opn2) - Sega Genesis/Mega Drive FM chip
- [YM2413 (OPLL)](/chips/fm/ym2413-opll) - Budget FM chip with preset instruments
- [YM2414 (OPZ)](/chips/fm/ym2414-opz) - Yamaha TX81Z chip
- [Y8950, YM3526, YM3812 (OPL)](/chips/fm/ym3526-opl) - AdLib and Sound Blaster chips
- [YMF262 (OPL3)](/chips/fm/ymf262-opl3) - Advanced OPL with 4-operator mode
- [YMF278 (OPL4)](/chips/fm/ymf278-opl4) - OPL3 combined with wavetable
- [ESFM](/chips/fm/esfm) - Enhanced FM synthesis chip

### Square Wave PSGs

Classic programmable sound generators that produce square waves, triangle waves, and noise. These define the sound of 8-bit gaming.

- [AY-3-8910/8914/YM2149(F)/Sunsoft 5B](/chips/square/ay8910) - Popular PSG used in MSX, ZX Spectrum, Amstrad CPC
- [Microchip AY8930](/chips/square/ay8930) - Enhanced AY-3-8910
- [SN76489/Sega PSG](/chips/square/sn76489) - Sega Master System and Genesis PSG
- [SAA1099](/chips/square/saa1099) - Philips PSG with 6 channels
- [MSM5232](/chips/square/msm5232) - 8-channel square wave generator

### Sample-Based Chips

These chips play back pre-recorded digital audio samples, either as PCM or compressed ADPCM.

- [Amiga Paula](/chips/sample/amiga) - 4-channel 8-bit PCM
- [SNES SPC700](/chips/sample/snes) - 8-channel ADPCM with DSP effects
- [Sega PCM](/chips/sample/segapcm) - Sega arcade PCM chip
- [QSound](/chips/sample/qsound) - Capcom CPS-2 surround sound
- [YMZ280B](/chips/sample/ymz280b) - 8-channel ADPCM with compression
- [RF5C68/RF5C164](/chips/sample/rf5c68) - Ricoh PCM chips
- [MSM6258](/chips/sample/msm6258) - OKI ADPCM voice synthesis
- [MSM6295](/chips/sample/msm6295) - 4-channel ADPCM used in many arcade games
- [K007232](/chips/sample/k007232) - Konami PCM chip
- [K053260](/chips/sample/k053260) - Konami arcade PCM
- [Irem GA20](/chips/sample/ga20) - 4-channel PCM
- [Ensoniq ES5506](/chips/sample/es5506) - 32-channel wavetable with filters
- [Namco C140](/chips/sample/c140) - 24-channel PCM
- [Namco C219](/chips/sample/c219) - 16-channel PCM

### Wavetable Chips

These chips use programmable waveforms to create custom timbres.

- [HuC6280 (PC Engine)](/chips/wavetable/huc6280) - 6-channel wavetable chip
- [Namco WSG/C15/C30](/chips/wavetable/namco-wsg) - Early Namco arcade wavetable
- [Konami SCC](/chips/wavetable/scc) - 5-channel wavetable used in MSX cartridges
- [WonderSwan](/chips/wavetable/wonderswan) - Bandai handheld wavetable chip
- [X1-010](/chips/wavetable/x1-010) - Seta wavetable chip
- [SM8521](/chips/wavetable/sm8521) - Game Boy-like chip with wavetable

- [2A03 (NES)](/chips/console/nes) - Nintendo Entertainment System / Famicom

- [Virtual Boy](/chips/console/virtual-boy) - 6-channel wavetable
- [Nintendo DS](/chips/console/nds) - 16-channel PCM
- [FDS](/chips/console/fds) - Famicom Disk System wavetable expansion
- [VRC6](/chips/console/vrc6) - Konami's Famicom expansion chip
- [VRC7](/chips/console/vrc7) - Konami's FM expansion for Famicom

- [Namco 163](/chips/console/n163) - Namco Famicom expansion
- [Watara Supervision](/chips/console/supervision) - Handheld console chip

- [TED](/chips/computer/ted) - Commodore Plus/4 and C16 sound
- [POKEY](/chips/computer/pokey) - Atari 8-bit computers
- [TIA](/chips/computer/tia) - Atari 2600/7800

- [PV-1000](/chips/computer/pv1000) - Casio console chip

Contemporary chips and imaginary sound processors designed for modern retro projects.

- [VERA](/chips/modern/vera) - Commander X16 modern retro chip
- [tildearrow Sound Unit](/chips/modern/sound-unit) - Fantasy chip by Furnace's creator
- [PowerNoise](/chips/modern/powernoise) - NES-inspired fantasy chip
- [Bifurcator](/chips/modern/bifurcator) - Unique phase distortion chip

- Sega Genesis (YM2612 + SN76489)
- Neo Geo (YM2610)
- NES with various expansion chips
- Arcade boards (Capcom CPS-1, CPS-2, etc.)
- And many more!
