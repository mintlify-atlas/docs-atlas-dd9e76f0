The Commodore VIC-20 was Commodore's major attempt at making a personal home computer, and is the precursor to the Commodore 64.

It was also known as the VC-20 in Germany, and the VIC-1001 in Japan.

## Sound Capabilities

The VIC-20 has 4 voices with a limited but wide tuning range. Like the SN76489 and T6W28, the last voice is dedicated to playing noise.

The 3 pulse wave channels have different octaves:
- **Channel 1**: Bass channel, plays notes from octave 1
- **Channel 2**: Mid/chord channel, plays notes from octave 2
- **Channel 3**: Lead channel, plays notes from octave 3

These channels are not referred to as "square" wave channels since a technique to play 15 additional pulse-like waveforms was discovered long after the VIC-20's release.

## Effect Commands

- `10xx`: **Switch waveform** (range: `00` to `0F`)

## Chip Configuration

**PAL**: Run at 1.10MHz instead of 1.02MHz

**Disable filtering**: Bypasses the hardware's lowpass filter
