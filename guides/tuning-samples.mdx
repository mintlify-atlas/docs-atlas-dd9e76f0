Loading a new sample into Furnace is easy, but getting it transposed and tuned to match the song can be tricky at first.

## Understanding Sample Tuning

<Warning>
  It's important to remember that the "Hz" and "Note" as shown in the sample editor are unrelated to the note heard in the sample itself. A sample shown as having a "Note" of C-4 will use a sample rate of 4181, even though it may contain a note played at a different pitch than C.
</Warning>

## Tuning Process

For this example, we'll use a sample of a note played at E and recorded at 22050Hz.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Instrument">
    If needed, use the "Create instrument from sample" button to make an instrument to use in the track.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Calculate Semitone Difference">
    Calculate the semitone difference in Hz between the note your recorded sample is playing and C.
    
    In this example, the nearest C is 4 semitones down from E.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Adjust Note Value">
    Set "Note" to 4 semitones lower than it shows.
    
    In this case, it starts at `F-6`, so set it to `C#6`.
    
    **Alternative method**: Use a pitch calculator like [Semitone Calculator](https://www.omnicalculator.com/other/semitone):
    - Input Frequency 1 as 22050Hz
    - Input -4 semitones
    - Receive a Frequency 2 of 17501.10Hz
    - Enter that value into "Hz"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test and Fine-Tune">
    Now, using the "Preview sample" button should play the note at C. Entering an E in the pattern will now play it at or near E.
    
    If the sample still sounds out of tune, adjust "Hz" or "Fine" to bring it in line.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Finding Sample Frequency

To find the frequency of a note:

1. Open the tuner (Window > Visualizers > Tuner)
2. When sound is played, the tuner will continually update to show the note closest to it
3. This corresponds to the highest peak shown in the spectrum analyzer (Window > Visualizers > Spectrum)

<Info>
  See the [Tuner](/advanced/tuner) documentation for more information on using the tuner.
</Info>

## Troubleshooting

### Notes Sound "Capped"

If notes seem "capped" (for example, playing anything over D-6 sounds like a D-6), those notes exceed the maximum sample playback rate for the chip.

<Warning>
  The only solution is to use "Resample" to change the sample to a lower rate.
</Warning>

### Samples Out of Tune in Pattern

If samples sound fine in the editor but out of tune when used in the track, there may be limitations on available playback rates.

<Info>
  Check the [chip's documentation](/chips/introduction) for details on sample playback limitations.
</Info>
