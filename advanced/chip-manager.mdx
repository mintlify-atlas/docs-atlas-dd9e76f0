title: Chip Manager
description: Add, remove, and configure sound chips in your module
---

The **chip manager** window allows you to manage chips, including adding, changing, moving or removing them.

![chip manager](/images/8-advanced/chip-manager.png)

## Global Options

<ParamField path="Preserve channel order" type="checkbox" default="false">
  Make existing pattern data stay in place even when chips are rearranged. When turned off, pattern data will be arranged to match (the default, and usually desired behavior).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Clone channel data" type="checkbox" default="false">
  When cloning chips, also copy patterns, pattern names, channel names and other parameters to the clone.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="Clone at end" type="checkbox" default="false">
  Instead of inserting the clone directly after the cloned chip, add it to the end.
</ParamField>

## Channel LEDs

Each chip shows an "LED" row with segments corresponding to its channels. They light up when a new note plays!

## Chip Controls

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Move Chip" icon="arrows-up-down">
    ![crossed-arrows](/images/8-advanced/chip-manager-move.png)
    
    Click and drag to reorder chips.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Clone Chip" icon="copy">
    ![two pages](/images/8-advanced/chip-manager-clone.png)
    
    Click to duplicate a chip with its settings.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Change Chip" icon="eject">
    ![eject](/images/8-advanced/chip-manager-change.png)
    
    Click to replace a chip with a different one. This displays the chip selector.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Remove Chip" icon="x">
    ![X](/images/8-advanced/chip-manager-remove.png)
    
    Click to remove a chip from the module.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Chip Configuration

Click the ![chevron](/images/8-advanced/chip-manager-edit.png) to the right of the LEDs to configure a chip. This allows you to change chip options such as:

- Clock rate
- Chip type/variant
- Other chip-specific settings

<Info>
  Refer to [each chip's documentation](/chips/introduction) for more information on chip-specific options.
</Info>
